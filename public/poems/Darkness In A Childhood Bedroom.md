## Darkness In A Childhood Bedroom

I can picture it all:
Me and my bride, hand in hand,
walking down the aisle on our way out of our wedding venue.
We finally chose one another for life.
To have and to hold, til death do us part.
She finally choosing me after realising she's been MY ONE for an entire year.

And later, me swinging our son around on a swing set in our backyard,
and playing dolls and house with our lovely daughter.

I'm fulfilled.

But the vision, it cuts to nothing, a darkness.

I think it's selfish and unfair that everyone wants me to stay alive for them, 
but no one wants to go through grief for me if I chose to end my own life,
while I've been grieving for people who are still alive, who've chosen others.
And I've been doing it for 14 years in the name of unrequited love.

I'm in a season of my life where I am once again not afraid of dying.
The whole day, I've been contemplating the perfect time to go Home to the Angels.

It's all because I couldn't capture her heart in time.
But I stayed, and she chose someone else twice.
And when I left, she chose someone else again.
Then I came back, and there was no her left for me to choose,
So I might choose death.
